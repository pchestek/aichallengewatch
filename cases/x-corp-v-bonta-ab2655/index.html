ACTIVE - Private challenge

Law Challenged:
California AB 2655 (Defending Democracy from Deepfake Deception Act)
Legal Theories:
  • First Amendment (prior restraint, compelled speech, editorial discretion)
  • Section 230 preemption
  • Dormant Commerce Clause

Summary

X Corp. (owner of the social media platform X, formerly Twitter) challenged AB 2655, which requires large online platforms to label or remove AI-generated election-related deepfakes. The law imposes a reporting and take-down regime for “deceptive content” created using generative AI.

X argues the law imposes unconstitutional prior restraints on speech, violates editorial discretion, and interferes with the platform’s content moderation policies. The case was consolidated with Kohls v. Bonta and other challenges.
